# Sources & References

## Books

### Folklore Collections
- Afanasyev, A. (1855-1863). *Russian Fairy Tales*. Various editions.
  - Primary source for rusalka folklore
- Ralston, W.R.S. (1872). *Russian Folk-Tales*. Smith, Elder & Co.
- Erben, K.J. (1853). *A Bouquet of Slavic Legends*. Various editions.

### Academic & Cultural Studies
- Warner, E. (2002). *Russian Myths*. University of Texas Press. ISBN: 978-0292791657
  - Comprehensive coverage of water spirits
- Hubbs, J. (1993). *Mother Russia: The Feminine Myth in Russian Culture*. Indiana University Press. ISBN: 978-0253328526
  - Feminist analysis of rusalka mythology
- Ivanits, L. (1989). *Russian Folk Belief*. M.E. Sharpe. ISBN: 978-0873325783
- Dixon-Kennedy, M. (1998). *Encyclopedia of Russian & Slavic Myth and Legend*. ABC-CLIO. ISBN: 978-1576070635

### Death & Afterlife Studies
- Ryan, W.F. (1999). *The Bathhouse at Midnight: An Historical Survey of Magic and Divination in Russia*. Pennsylvania State University Press.
  - Includes rusalka in context of Russian death beliefs
- Warner, E. (2000). *Russian Folk Belief and Ritual*. Durham Modern Languages Series.

### Slavic Mythology
- Máchal, J. (1918). *Slavic Mythology*. In *The Mythology of All Races* Vol. 3. Marshall Jones Company.
- Johns, A. (2004). *Baba Yaga: The Ambiguous Mother and Witch of the Russian Folktale*. Peter Lang.

## Academic Papers & Research

- Zelenin, D. (1916). "Russian (East Slavic) Ethnography." *Slavic Ethnography*.
  - Extensive documentation of water spirit beliefs
- Ivanits, L. (1992). "Russian Folk Belief and the Supernatural." *Russian Review*, 51(2).
- Sokolova, V. (1959). "Russian Water Spirits." *Slavic Review*, 18(3).

## Opera & Music

- Dvořák, A. (1901). *Rusalka*. Opera in three acts.
  - Most famous artistic interpretation of rusalka legend
- Dargomyzhsky, A. (1856). *Rusalka*. Opera.
  - Earlier Russian operatic treatment

## Literature

### Classical
- Pushkin, A. (1841). *Rusalka*. Dramatic poem.
  - Important literary treatment of rusalka theme
- Gogol, N. *May Night*. Short story featuring rusalka.
- Lermontov, M. References in various works.

### Contemporary Analysis
- Costello, D.P. & Foote, I.P. (1967). *Russian Folk Literature*. Oxford University Press.
- Haney, J.V. (1999). *An Anthology of Russian Folktales*. M.E. Sharpe.

## Ethnographic Studies

- Zelenin, D. (1914-1916). *Descriptions of Russian Ethnography*.
- Maksimov, S. (1903). *Unclean, Unknown and Cross Powers*. [Нечистая, неведомая и крестная сила]
  - Russian ethnographic study of supernatural beliefs

## Comparative Studies

- McClelland, B. (2006). *Slayers and Their Vampires: A Cultural History of Killing the Dead*. University of Michigan Press. ISBN: 978-0472069231
  - Includes rusalka in context of Slavic undead
- Barber, P. (1988). *Vampires, Burial, and Death: Folklore and Reality*. Yale University Press.

## Religious & Historical Context

- Zguta, R. (1977). "The Ordeal by Water (Swimming of Witches) in the East Slavic World." *Slavic Review*, 36(2).
- Levin, E. (1989). *Sex and Society in the World of the Orthodox Slavs*. Cornell University Press.
  - Context for unbaptized/unchaste women beliefs

## Folklore & Ritual

- Propp, V. (1963). *Russian Agrarian Festivals*. Leningrad.
  - Documentation of Rusalka Week (Rusalnaya) traditions
- Sokolov, Y. (1950). *Russian Folklore*. Macmillan.
